Featured Projects

1. Retail Sales & Inventory Performance Dashboard

Power BI Excel SQL Retail Analytics KPI Reporting

Business Problem: Retail teams need clear visibility of sales performance, stock movement, category trends, and reorder risks. Without structured reporting, slow-moving products, stock gaps, and category performance issues can be missed.

Objective: Analyse retail sales and inventory data to identify top-performing categories, slow-moving products, stock risk, and opportunities to improve replenishment decisions.

Data Used: Retail-style data including product ID, product name, category, brand, units sold, revenue, stock available, reorder level, supplier, and store location.

Analysis Completed:

  • Cleaned product and stock data by checking missing values, duplicate SKUs, inconsistent categories, and incorrect price/revenue fields.
  • Created sales, revenue, stock status, reorder risk, and category performance metrics.
  • Built dashboard views for executive summary, category performance, inventory movement, and action recommendations.
  • Identified fast-moving products, slow-moving products, low-stock items, and overstocked categories.

Key Insights: The analysis highlights which categories drive revenue, which products need reorder attention, and which stock lines should be reviewed for pricing, promotion, or reduced purchasing.

Business Value: Supports better stock control, faster reporting, improved product visibility, and more confident operational decisions.

2. Service Operations & Incident Analysis

SQL Power BI Excel Jira-style Data Root Cause Analysis

Business Problem: Service teams need to understand incident trends, repeated issues, downtime, and resolution performance. Without clear reporting, recurring problems continue and service performance becomes reactive instead of controlled.

Objective: Analyse incident and service operations data to identify issue patterns, resolution bottlenecks, recurring incident categories, and opportunities to improve service continuity.

Data Used: Incident records including ticket ID, issue category, priority, status, created date, resolved date, resolution time, impacted system, root cause, and recurrence flag.

Analysis Completed:

  • Grouped incidents by category, priority, system, status, and recurrence pattern.
  • Calculated incident volume, average resolution time, downtime trend, and recurring issue rate.
  • Built reporting views to highlight top issue categories and delayed resolution areas.
  • Used root cause analysis to separate isolated issues from repeat operational problems.

Key Insights: The analysis identifies high-frequency issue categories, repeated incidents, and areas where better documentation, monitoring, or escalation rules could reduce service disruption.

Business Value: Demonstrates practical service analytics, incident reporting, and data-led operational improvement.

3. Employee Attrition Analysis

Python Excel Power BI Predictive Analytics HR Analytics

Business Problem: Organisations need to understand why employees leave and which factors increase attrition risk. Without structured analysis, retention actions become generic and ineffective.

Objective: Analyse employee attrition data to identify key drivers of turnover and recommend actions to improve retention.

Data Used: HR-style dataset containing employee demographics, department, job role, salary, tenure, satisfaction level, performance rating, overtime, and attrition status.

Analysis Completed:

  • Cleaned and prepared employee data for analysis by handling missing values and standardising categorical variables.
  • Explored attrition patterns by department, role, salary band, tenure, overtime, and satisfaction level.
  • Created visual summaries to show the strongest attrition risk areas.
  • Built a predictive model to classify attrition risk and explain the most important influencing factors.

Key Insights: Attrition risk is often linked to a combination of workload, satisfaction, tenure, salary progression, and department-level patterns rather than one single cause.

Business Value: Helps HR and management teams target retention actions using evidence instead of assumptions.

4. Customer Sales & Revenue Analysis

Excel SQL Power BI Revenue Analysis Customer Insights

Business Problem: Businesses need to know where revenue comes from, which customer segments perform best, and what actions can increase repeat sales and average order value.

Objective: Analyse customer sales data to understand revenue trends, customer segments, product performance, repeat purchasing, and growth opportunities.

Data Used: Sales records including order ID, customer ID, customer segment, product category, order date, units sold, price, revenue, region, and repeat purchase flag.

Analysis Completed:

  • Calculated monthly revenue, average order value, repeat purchase rate, and product/category performance.
  • Segmented customers by purchase frequency, revenue contribution, and category preference.
  • Identified high-value customer groups and underperforming product categories.
  • Created business recommendations for pricing, promotions, and customer retention.

Key Insights: Revenue growth is usually driven by a small group of high-value customers and strong-performing categories, while low-performing segments require targeted action.

Business Value: Shows ability to connect sales data with commercial decision-making and business growth recommendations.
